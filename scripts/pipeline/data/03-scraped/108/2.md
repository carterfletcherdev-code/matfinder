 What is it
 ## What Is Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu? Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a grappling-based martial art focused on takedowns, positional control, and submissions. The core principle: technique and leverage beat size and strength, which means anyone can learn it, and it actually works.
 At Bittencourt BJJ, we train both Gi (with the kimono) and No-Gi (shorts and rash guard). Beginners are always welcome. Every black belt started exactly where you are now. Learn more about the art at the International Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Federation (IBJJF) .

 Common questions
 ## Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu FAQ Do I need any experience to start? None at all. We welcome complete beginners every day. Your first class is free. Just show up and we’ll take care of the rest.

 What should I wear to my first class? Athletic shorts and a t-shirt are perfect for your free trial. Once you decide to join, you’ll need a Gi (kimono) and we can point you in the right direction.

 Is BJJ safe? Will I get hurt? BJJ is one of the safest martial arts you can train. We emphasize controlled sparring, proper technique, and respect between training partners. Beginners always start light.

 How often should I train? 2 to 3 times per week is ideal for steady progress. Some students train more, some less. The important thing is consistency over time.

 Do you offer classes for women? Yes. We have a dedicated Women-Only Gi class, a safe and supportive environment to learn BJJ alongside other women. All levels welcome.
