 # Progress Over Perfection, the Ted Lasso Approach to Coaching Jiu Jitsu
 December 3, 2025
 Beginner Guides , Kids Jiu Jitsu , Martial Arts

 Ted Lasso may be a fictional football coach with questionable knowledge of the actual sport… but his attitude ? That’s the real black-belt material.
 At Veritas, we say this with love and zero apology: if your coach isn’t inspiring you to become a better person on and off the mats — if they don’t believe in you louder than you believe in yourself — you might be at the wrong gym.
 Jiu Jitsu is more than technique; it’s growth, confidence, resilience, and the people who help pull that out of you. A coach should lift you, challenge you, support you, and push you toward the version of yourself you’re trying to become.

 Ted’s whole thing isn’t perfect technique or a flawless track record. The dude literally didn’t know the rules of soccer. His superpower is the way he makes people better. He brings positivity, belief, and humor into pressure-filled moments — and suddenly the people around him start leveling up.
 Sound familiar?
 Because that’s exactly what great Jiu Jitsu coaching looks like.
 A good coach corrects your mistakes.
 But a great coach builds you up, sees your potential, and keeps you moving forward even when you’re discouraged, exhausted, or — let’s say hypothetically — leading with your head and getting almost put twice in one round.
 And that’s where our head coach, Justin Overton , drops the line that’s basically tattooed on everyone’s brain around here:

 PROGRESS OVER PERFECTION.
 It doesn’t matter if you messed up a guard pass.
 It doesn’t matter if you got caught.
 It doesn’t matter if your technique looked like a confused baby giraffe.
 What matters is: you showed up, you learned something, and you’re better than you were yesterday.
 Ted Lasso energy on the mats means:
 – Encouragement that’s real, not fluffy.
 – Accountability without shame.
 – Belief in your potential — especially when you don’t see it yet.
 – A coaching style where you leave feeling capable, not crushed.
 – A vibe that pushes you forward without demanding perfection
 And here’s the truth:
 If your gym doesn’t have that kind of atmosphere — if the coaches don’t care about your growth, your confidence, your journey, you — then yeah… it might be time to find somewhere that does.
 At Veritas, we’re not chasing perfection.
 We’re chasing progress, consistency, and community.
 And maybe a little Ted Lasso-style optimism sprinkled in — minus the British biscuits, ok sometimes we add biscuits.
