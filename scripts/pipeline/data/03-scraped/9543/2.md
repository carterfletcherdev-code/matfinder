 ## QUESTIONS TEMPE RESIDENTS ASK I have never done any martial arts. Can I still start?
 Absolutely. The majority of our members started with zero experience. Every class is structured so beginners can learn alongside experienced students. You will drill at your own pace and nobody expects you to know anything on day one.

 How often should I train as a beginner?
 Two to three times per week is ideal for beginners. That gives your body time to recover while building enough consistency to retain techniques. Most members settle into a rhythm within the first few weeks.

 Will I get injured?
 BJJ is one of the safest martial arts because there is no striking. The most common issues are minor — mat burns, sore muscles, the occasional jammed finger. We emphasize controlled technique and tapping early, which prevents serious injuries.

 What is the difference between Gi and No-Gi?
 Gi classes use the traditional kimono, which allows grip-based techniques and a more methodical pace. No-Gi is faster, more athletic, and translates more directly to real-world scenarios. We recommend trying both.

 Do I need to be in shape first?
 No. You will get in shape by training. We have had members start at every fitness level. The first few weeks will be challenging, but your conditioning improves dramatically once you show up consistently.

 Is there sparring in every class?
 Most classes include some form of live training. As a beginner, participation in live rounds is always optional until you feel ready. Nobody will force you to spar.
