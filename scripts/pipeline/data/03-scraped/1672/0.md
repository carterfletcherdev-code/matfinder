## All Disciplines Open Mat at American Jujitsu Our All Disciplines Open Mat at American Jujitsu is the perfect opportunity for athletes of all backgrounds to come together, collaborate, and sharpen their skills in a relaxed, drama-free environment. Held weekly at our Springfield, IL location, this free training session welcomes practitioners from any gym, style, or experience level. Whether you train in Jujitsu, MMA, Kickboxing, Wrestling, or another discipline, Open Mat offers the space and freedom to train the way you want.
 Unlike structured classes, Open Mat is student-driven—providing time for sparring, drilling, technique sharing, or light flow rolls at your own pace. It’s a great way to reinforce what you've learned, explore cross-training opportunities, and connect with a broader martial arts community. With mats open and instructors available for guidance, the session encourages growth through exploration, respectful challenge, and open exchange of knowledge.
 This hour is perfect for anyone who wants extra mat time, wants to test techniques in live settings, or simply wants to train in a social and supportive space. Whether you're a beginner looking to get more comfortable or an advanced student fine-tuning your game, you'll find something valuable here. Plus, it’s a great way to meet training partners from various disciplines and backgrounds.

 How long does it take to see results in BJJ?

 Results in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu take time and dedication, but with consistent training, you'll notice improvements. Beginners often see progress within the first month, including enhanced strength, technique, and overall fitness as you adapt to the demands of the mat.

 Do I get the chance to meet new friends through BJJ?

 Absolutely! Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu fosters a strong sense of community. In our small group classes, you'll have plenty of opportunities to meet and train with fellow practitioners. You'll not only build friendships but also create a supportive network as you learn and grow together on the mats.

 What sets you apart from other BJJ academies?

 At our academy, you're not just joining a gym; you're becoming part of a close-knit community that shares your passion for Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. We believe in lifting each other up—when you succeed on the mats, we all celebrate together. Our supportive environment encourages personal growth, skill development, and lasting friendships, making your BJJ journey truly unique.

 How often do you recommend training in BJJ classes?

 For beginners in BJJ, we recommend starting with 2 to 4 classes per week. This allows you to gradually adapt to the techniques and physical demands of the sport. As you build your skills and conditioning, aim to work up to 5 classes a week after your initial months. Remember, BJJ is both physically and mentally challenging, so it's important to listen to your body and incorporate recovery time between training sessions to ensure optimal progress and avoid burnout. Consistency is key, but quality training sessions are essential for your growth on the mats!

 What should I wear for BJJ Training?

 Our students usually wear a gi (uniform) for traditional classes or rash guards and spats for no-gi sessions. It's important to choose high-quality gear that fits well and meets competition standards, which is why Sagaz offers all the gear you need (at additional cost). But we also lend out gi's for your free trial, so just come in athletic clothing, and we'll take care of the rest!

 Do I have to be physically fit to start BJJ?

 No! You don’t need to be fit to start Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. Our program is designed for all fitness levels, and every class will help you improve your fitness, technique, and confidence on the mats. Whether you're a complete beginner or have some experience, our training is universally scalable, ensuring that anyone dedicated to learning can succeed and progress.
