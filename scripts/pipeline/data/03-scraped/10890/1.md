 ## What the First Year of Training Actually Looks Like

 Most people have no idea what to expect when they start. Here is an honest picture.
 The First Few Weeks You are learning how to move. How to fall. How to stay calm when someone is close and applying pressure. It feels like a lot. That is completely normal. Everyone in that room felt exactly the same thing in their first few weeks.
 The First Few Months Something starts to click. You recognize positions. You understand what your partner is trying to do before they do it. You stop reacting and start thinking. That shift is one of the most satisfying things you will experience in this gym.
 Six Months to a Year You have a game. You have positions you prefer and positions you are working to improve. You have started wanting to compete, or decided you do not need to, and you are fine either way. The people you started training with have become some of the most consistent people in your life.
 Belt promotions at 2nd Gear are not on a schedule. They happen when your coach sees consistent improvement in your movement, your decision-making, and your understanding of the art. Most students earn their blue belt somewhere between one and three years depending on how consistently they train.
 There is no shortcut. There is also no ceiling.

 ## Common Questions

 Do I need to be in shape first? No. Training is how you get in shape. Students start here at every fitness level.
 Is there a class specifically for beginners? Yes. The fundamentals class is built for people who are new. You will not be placed in an advanced class before you are ready.
 Do I need to buy a gi before my first class? No. We have loaner gis available. Come in athletic clothes and we take care of the rest.
 What if I have an injury or a bad knee? Let the coach know before class. Most conditions can be worked around. Jiu jitsu is built on technique and leverage, not on physical strain.
 Is there sparring right away? Not in the fundamentals class. You learn positions and drill techniques first. Nobody is put into live rolling before they are ready.
 How often should I train? Two to three times per week is a solid starting point. Consistency matters more than volume, especially in the first few months.
