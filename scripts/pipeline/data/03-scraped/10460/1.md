 ## JIU-JITSU PROGRAMS LITTLE PORCUPINES ~ages 4-6​Little Porcupines is a special course designed to help pre-school and early elementary aged children learn in a fun, safe manner. These young students use games (drills) to:Develop coordination and confidenceGrow comfortable with appropriate physical contactLearn how to speak with strength and respect​Handle challenges and difficult situations with courage​Mondays & Wednesdays: 4:30pm-5:00pmFridays: 5:15pm-5:45pm | SCHEDULE A VISIT

 YOUTH LEVEL 1 ~ages 7-10​ Our Juniors program is specifically designed for elementary age kids. This course focuses on:Learning to stand and speak with strength and confidenceRespectfully establishing boundaries and then enforcing themDeveloping strong self-control so that we respond rather than reactCore self-defense skills to stop others from hurting us while doing as little harm as possible​​Mondays & Wednesdays: 5:00pm-5:45pm Fridays: 5:45pm-6:30pm | SCHEDULE A VISIT

 YOUTH LEVEL 2 ~ages 11-15​Our youth class is designed for kids who are approaching adulthood. This program is physically and mentally more demanding and builds on what has been taught in previous courses including:How to handle more aggressive physical violenceHow to defend against larger, stronger aggressorsHow to make good choices that lead to safer, happier lives​Mondays & Wednesdays: 5:45pm-6:30pm Fridays: 5:45pm-6:30pm | SCHEDULE A VISIT

 ADULTS ~ages 15+​The Adult Jiu-Jitsu course is a phenomenal fitness and self-defense program designed for men and women of all ages and all skill levels. Classes are designed to promote:Healthy fitness, nutrition, and aging.Sharp, effective self-defense skillsA community of teammates who work together toward their goalsMondays 6:30-7:30pm (Intro)Mondays 7:30-9pm (Advanced)Wednesdays, & Fridays 6:30pm-8:00pm​Tuesdays & Thursdays 12:00pm-1:30pmSaturdays 10:00am-11:00am | SCHEDULE A VISIT

 | COMPETITION TRAINING​SOMA Academy is a self-defense based academy. Our primary goals are to learn to defend ourselves and to become better people. However, for those who would like to compete, additional training is offered to help students prepare for Jiu-Jitsu competition. All students may attend these training classes, even if they do not wish to compete.​
