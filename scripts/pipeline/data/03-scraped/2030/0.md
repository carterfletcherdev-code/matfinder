 ## Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu FAQ's Expand FAQ's 1. What can BJJ and your school do for me?
 Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu builds real confidence, functional strength, and mental discipline. Training consistently improves your conditioning, mobility, focus, stress management, and overall mindset. Students also gain a powerful sense of community — you’re not just joining a gym, you’re joining a culture built around respect, accountability, and personal growth. Whether your goal is self-defense, fitness, competition, or a life change, BJJ gives you measurable progress month after month.
 2. Do I need to be in shape to start?
 No. BJJ gets you in shape. Classes scale to your level — beginners start slow, learn positioning, drills, and controlled technique. You’ll build cardio naturally through training, not by needing it beforehand. Everyone starts at zero.
 3. Am I too old?
 Absolutely not. We have students in their teens, 20s, 30s, 40s, 50s, and beyond. BJJ is a lifelong martial art that adapts to your body and your pace. Your age is not a barrier — your consistency is.
 4. Will I be expected to compete?
 Competition is optional. You will never be pushed to compete unless you want to. We offer competition training for those who choose that path, but regular classes are designed for all skill levels and all goals — lifestyle, fitness, knowledge, or self-defense.
 5. Do I need prior martial arts experience?
 None. Most students start with zero experience. The curriculum is structured for beginners first, then builds layer by layer into advanced concepts. You learn fundamentals at your own pace.
 6. Do you offer beginner-friendly classes?
 Yes. We have structured classes that teach the core positions, movements, transitions, and submissions in a controlled, easy-to-absorb format. Instructors guide every step and ensure beginners never feel overwhelmed.
 7. Do you offer striking classes?
 Yes — we also offer Kickboxing, Muay Thai, and MMA. These classes complement BJJ perfectly and are excellent for cardio, timing, confidence, and self-defense.
 8. Will BJJ help me lose weight?
 Extremely. Rolling, drilling, and class structure burn calories fast and build lean muscle. Most students see changes in weight, energy, and confidence within the first few weeks — BJJ is one of the most effective and enjoyable forms of fitness you can commit to.
 9. What does a typical class look like?
 Classes usually follow this structure:
 • Warm-up: mobility, movement patterns, light conditioning.
 • Technique: step-by-step instruction, drilling, learning positions.
 • Live training: situational training → controlled rolls → optional sparring.
 Each class builds on the previous one, creating a steady progression in skill and understanding.
 10. Do you offer family or multi-student discounts?
 Yes. We support families training together and offer reduced pricing for households with multiple members training.
 11. How much does membership cost?
 Pricing varies depending on training goals, programs, and membership type. We offer multiple options — adults, kids, full access, and specialty programs. Contact us directly for the most accurate plan based on what you want to achieve.
 12. What age can kids start?
 Kids classes start at age 5. The program builds confidence, discipline, coordination, bully-proofing, and respect — all while teaching real martial arts skills in a safe, structured environment.
 13. How long does it take to earn a Black Belt?
 BJJ is earned, not given. On average, it takes 8–12 years depending on consistency, attitude, effort, and dedication. There are no shortcuts — every belt represents real skill and real work.
 14. How do I get started?
 Schedule your free class, show up with comfortable athletic clothing, and be ready to learn. You can also call 253-254-6538 to speak directly with the academy.
