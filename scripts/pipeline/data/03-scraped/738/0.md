# Bristol Jiu Jitsu Club
 ## ABOUT US We are part of The Jiu Jitsu Foundation (TJJF) who are the largest single style Jiu Jitsu organisation in Britain.
 At Bristol Jiu Jitsu Club we practice Japanese Jiu Jitsu in a modern setting, emphasizing its use as a tool for self defence.
 As a martial art we utilise throws, joint locks, strikes and much more to neutralise an opponent in a wide variety of threatening scenarios. Not only will you learn to apply these techniques in a 1-on-1 setting, you’ll develop strategies for dealing with multiple attackers and opponents with an array of common weapons.

 ## WHAT WE PRACTICE ### Throws We teach a lot of throwing techniques in Jiu Jitsu with each one having pros and cons in different situations. They require lots of practice to be used effectively but you’ll be able to learn and experience them for yourself from your first session.

 ### Joint Locks There are many strategies to dealing with an attacker. Wrist locks, arm locks and ground immobilisations are effective methods of restraint without the need for striking. These are a key part of our Jiu Jitsu.

 ### Weapon Work The use of a weapon can provide an attacker with an immediate and seemingly overwhelming advantage. For the times confrontation is unavoidable, we practice defences from a variety of weapons, particularly bottles, coshes and knifes.

 ### Breakfalling In order to teach effective throwing, it is critical we learn how to fall safely. Training safely is at the heart of everything we do at Bristol Jiu Jitsu Club so breakfalling is practiced at the start of every session.
