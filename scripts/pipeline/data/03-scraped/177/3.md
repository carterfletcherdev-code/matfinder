# PROGRAMS
 #### #### Tumblers (3-4 years old): The Tumblers class is specially designed for 3-4-year-olds as a fun and safe introduction to Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. This beginner program helps children develop motor skills, strength, and balance through engaging physical activities. Kids will learn basic movements like break falls and forward tumbles, while also being taught valuable lessons such as respect, patience, teamwork, and overcoming challenges. To ensure a safe environment, students don’t practice techniques on each other but instead grapple with the instructors, making the class both enjoyable and secure for young learners.
 Uniform: Gi
 Little Grapplers (5-7 years old):
 The Little Grapplers class is designed for children ages 5 to 7 and focuses on teaching the fundamental techniques of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu in a structured, engaging environment. At this age, students begin interacting with one another, learning to apply techniques with partners through controlled drills and games. They also start facing new challenges that promote problem-solving, coordination, and confidence. You can expect kids in this group to develop better focus, improved listening skills, and a growing sense of discipline. This stage lays the groundwork for understanding timing, position, and cooperation—essential elements for their BJJ journey and personal growth.
 Uniform: Gi
 #### Youth BJJ (8-12 years old): The Youth BJJ class for ages 8 to 12 is focused on building strong technical foundations and preparing students for more advanced aspects of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. At this age, kids are ready to learn and apply techniques with greater precision, practice live drills, and begin light sparring in a safe and structured setting. They’ll develop critical thinking skills as they encounter more complex situations, learning to adapt and solve problems on the mat. Students also grow in confidence, discipline, and resilience—key traits that benefit them both in training and everyday life. Respect, teamwork, and a strong work ethic are consistently emphasized.
 Uniform: Gi
 #### Teenagers (13-17 years old): he Teen BJJ class for ages 13 to 17 is designed to refine technical skills, deepen understanding of strategy, and introduce students to a higher level of training intensity. At this stage, teens practice full techniques, positional sparring, and controlled live rolling, preparing them for both recreational and competitive goals. This age group is capable of more focused effort, critical analysis, and physical endurance. Classes also emphasize accountability, leadership, and perseverance—helping teens build confidence, manage stress, and stay committed through challenges. Whether they’re training for competition or personal growth, this class offers a supportive path forward in their Jiu-Jitsu journey.
 Uniform: Gi
 #### BRAZILIAN JIU-JITSU FOR ADULTS (16+ years old) Students will learn and practice the fundamentals of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, including mat exercises, grappling techniques, and submission holds in an inspiring and respectful environment. Mixed-level classes give practitioners the opportunity to drill both basic and advanced moves, developing a wide-range of skills through partnership with both beginners and experienced practitioners. An excellent full-body workout and self-defense system for all, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu has been known to reduce stress, boost energy, improve strength and conditioning, increase flexibility and mobility, enhance self-esteem, and more. We currently offer mixed-level Gi and No Gi classes. While some students prefer one style over another, many practitioners train in Gi as well as No Gi, enjoying the benefits and advantages of both.
 Gi: Gi classes use a traditional BJJ Gi, incorporating various grips on both the jacket and pants within many BJJ techniques.
 Uniform: Gi
 No Gi: No Gi students typically wear a rashguard and shorts/spats to train. No Gi involves more wrestling-style grips (as there are no clothing grips) and can be faster paced as a sport.
 Uniform: Rashguard, shorts/spats
 #### #### WOMEN’S BRAZILIAN JIU JITSU Women in this class will learn effective and realistic self-defense techniques in a safe, clean, and respectful environment. Training focuses on real-world applications for both sport and self-defense, empowering students with practical skills while boosting confidence and self-esteem. In addition to learning how to stay safe, students will benefit from improved fitness, mobility, and agility through a full-body workout that also helps reduce stress and build mental resilience.
 #### PRIVATE LESSONS For students looking to reach their highest potential in martial arts, private lessons are highly recommended. These one-on-one sessions allow instructors to provide personalized attention that isn’t possible in a group setting, tailoring each lesson to the student’s unique goals, strengths, and areas for improvement. Private training also helps instructors build a deeper connection with the student and closely track their progress both on and off the mat. Whether you’re aiming to sharpen specific techniques, prepare for competition, or accelerate your overall development, private lessons offer the fastest and most focused path forward.
