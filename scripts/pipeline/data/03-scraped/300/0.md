 ## Frequently Asked Questions ### What age is the best to start jiu jitsu? Kids can start as young as 4 through our Young Kids Fundamentals class , which focuses on basic movement, coordination, and listening skills. Our main Kids Jiu Jitsu program begins at age 7, where students can start learning real technique on a clear beginner-to-advanced pathway. There's no wrong age to begin — the earlier they start, the sooner they will build the habits of discipline and confidence that carry into everything else.
 ### Is jiu jitsu safe for kids? Yes. Jiu jitsu is one of the safest martial arts for kids because it's built on grappling and control rather than strikes. At Shimeru, we train on professional Dollamur tatami mats with a spring-loaded subfloor specifically designed to protect joints and absorb impact. Beginner classes spend weeks on fundamentals before introducing any controlled sparring.
 ### What's the difference between jiu jitsu, BJJ and judo? Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (BJJ) focuses primarily on ground grappling – positions, submissions, and escapes. Judo emphasizes throws and takedowns, with some ground work. Both are grappling arts that complement each other perfectly, which is why Shimeru teaches a curriculum that blends the best of both traditions. Your child will become a more complete martial artist here than at a school that teaches only one.
 ### How often should my child train? We recommend starting with 2 classes per week to build consistency without overwhelming your child. As they progress and show interest, 3 to 4 classes per week is ideal for steady advancement through the beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels. Kids who train consistently typically see noticeable improvements in confidence, focus, and physical skill within the first two or three months. If your kid finds his or her passion in jiu jitsu and wants to train more often, we recommend you absolutely support that.
 ### What if my child is shy or has never done sports before? That's one of the most common starting points for kids who join Shimeru. Our beginner classes are designed specifically for newcomers — no athletic background required. Coaches pair kids with supportive training partners and keep the environment encouraging, so shy kids usually open up within their first few sessions once they realize everyone started exactly where they are.
 ### What does my child need to wear or bring to their first class? For their free trial week, kids can come in athletic clothing like a t-shirt and shorts or sweatpants — no uniform needed yet. Bring a water bottle, and make sure fingernails and toenails are trimmed short for mat safety. If your child decides to continue after the trial, we'll help you get a properly fitted gi (uniform) before they need it.
 ### How does the belt ranking system work for kids? Our kids program uses a colored-belt and stripe progression that rewards consistent effort, improvement, and character — not just technical skill. Students typically earn stripes every few months when training consistently and advance belts over longer periods, celebrating real milestones at each step. The goal isn't to rush through ranks but to build genuine skill and maturity at every level.
