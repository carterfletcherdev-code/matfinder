 FAQ
 ## Common Questions
 Do I need any experience to start? Absolutely not! Most of our students started with zero martial arts experience. Our Beginners and Essentials classes are specifically designed for newcomers. You'll be guided every step of the way by patient, experienced black belt instructors.

 What should I wear to my first class? For your first class, just wear comfortable athletic clothing — shorts or joggers and a t-shirt or rashguard. We provide loaner gis for Gi classes so you can try before you buy. No shoes needed on the mat!

 What age can my child start training? Our Little Axes program accepts children starting at age 4. Classes are designed to be fun and age-appropriate, focusing on coordination, confidence, and basic self-defense through games and structured activities. Kids ages 8–15 join our Strong Axes program for more structured training.

 What's the difference between Gi and No-Gi? Gi Jiu-Jitsu uses the traditional kimono (gi), which adds grips and a more technical, chess-like element. No-Gi uses shorts and a rashguard — it's faster-paced and more wrestling-oriented. Both develop different skills and we recommend training both. Many of our classes offer each style throughout the week.

 Is Jiu-Jitsu safe for my child? Yes! Our kids programs emphasize safety above everything else. Techniques are age-appropriate, and our instructors carefully supervise all activities. BJJ is actually one of the safest martial arts for children because it focuses on control and submissions rather than striking. It also teaches kids to protect themselves in real-world situations.

 How much does membership cost? We offer several membership options to fit different budgets and training goals, including family discounts. The best way to learn about pricing is to come in for your free trial class — we'll walk you through all options with zero pressure. Contact us at (214) 498-7437 or fill out the form on this page.
