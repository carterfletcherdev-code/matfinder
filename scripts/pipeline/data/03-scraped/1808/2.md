 ### Do I need prior martial arts or self-defense experience to join?

 No experience is needed! Our instructors teach fundamentals and help students progress at their own pace in a welcoming environment.

 ### How does Brazilian Jiu Jitsu compare to karate or other martial arts?

 BJJ focuses on ground techniques, submissions, and real-world self-defense, emphasizing technique over strength. It’s excellent for building confidence, discipline, and practical skills. Karate is typically more striking-based. BJJ is widely regarded as highly effective for self-defense, fitness, and sport.

 - - - -

 ### What do students need to wear?

 For their first few classes, we encourage you to wear comfortable athletic clothes. For gi classes, students will eventually want a gi uniform. No-gi classes require athletic gear like rash guards and shorts.

 - - - -

 ### What’s the difference between gi and no-gi classes?

 Gi classes use the traditional uniform, allowing grips on the clothing for technique. No-gi classes are done in athletic wear without the gi, focusing more on speed and different grips. Both offer excellent training benefits.

 - - - -

 ### How often should I train to improve my Brazilian Jiu Jitsu skills and fitness?

 Training 2–3 times weekly is ideal for skill progression, fitness gains, and confidence. Even once a week provides great health and self-defense benefits, but we are flexible to fit you and your family’s schedule.
