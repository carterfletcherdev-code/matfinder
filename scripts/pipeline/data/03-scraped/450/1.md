# Why BJJ is Great for teaching Kids Commitment and Resilience
 Nov 29 Written By Mike Martin

 In today's fast-paced world, teaching children the value of commitment and perseverance can be a challenge. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) is an excellent martial art that instills these qualities in children from a young age. With its focus on strategy, discipline, and the importance of never giving up, BJJ provides numerous benefits that extend beyond the physical aspects of the sport. In this article, we will explore why BJJ is great for kids and provide strategies for talking to children about commitment.
 Strong Heart BJJ Teaches Kids to Stick with Something.
 One of the most valuable lessons that children can learn through practicing BJJ is the importance of sticking with something, even when it becomes difficult. BJJ techniques and moves require precision, patience, and determination to master. It is common for children to encounter challenges and setbacks along their BJJ journey, which presents an ideal opportunity for them to develop resilience.
 By persistently facing obstacles on the BJJ mats, children learn that setbacks are merely stepping stones towards improvement. This mindset translates into other areas of their lives, encouraging them to embrace challenges instead of giving up easily. Whether it's academic performance, extracurricular activities, or personal goals, BJJ empowers children to tackle adversity head-on, fostering a strong sense of commitment.
 Strategies for Talking to Kids about Commitment
 1. Lead by example: Children often imitate the behaviors and attitudes of their parents or role models. Demonstrating commitment in your own endeavors, whether it be work, hobbies, or personal growth, will inspire your child to do the same.
 2. Encourage open communication: Create a safe space where your child feels comfortable discussing their fears, doubts, and frustrations. By empathizing with their emotions and actively listening, you can help them develop strategies to overcome challenges.
 3. Highlight the long-term benefits: Talk to your child about the rewards that come with perseverance and commitment. Explain how sticking with something often leads to improvement, growth, and a sense of accomplishment.
 4. Break down goals: Encourage your child to set achievable goals within their BJJ journey. Help them create a roadmap, outlining small milestones along the way. Celebrating each accomplishment will motivate them to continue their commitment.
 5. Reinforce the importance of patience: Teach your child that mastery takes time and effort. Emphasize that patience is a virtue and that the journey is just as important as reaching the destination.
 Strong Heart Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu offers children a fantastic opportunity to learn commitment and resilience, qualities that are essential for success in many aspects of life. Through the challenges and triumphs of BJJ training, children develop a tenacious mindset and an unwavering commitment to their goals. By implementing the strategies mentioned above, parents and mentors can guide children on their journey and foster a lifelong dedication to embracing challenges head-on.
