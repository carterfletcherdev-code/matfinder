# Going beyond the basics

 ## At Trein Fort Bend you train with the best
 All individuals qualified to teach at Trein are instructors, this includes Masters, Professors, Head Instructors, Chief Instructors, and Coaches. Trein Club Instructors are held to high standards of etiquette, instruction, and philosophy, rooted in the holistic vision of our founder, Pedro.
 To preserve the integrity of our training environment and ensure the highest level of safety, all instructors at Trein Fort Bend are certified. In addition, every instructor is required to hold valid CPR and Basic First Aid certification.
 At Trein, expertise, care, and consistency come together to support your journey, on and off the mats.

 ###### Cassio Francis
 ###### Black Belt World Champion
 Cassio Francis, affectionately known in the jiu-jitsu world as Cassão, was born on June 18, 1983, in Belo Horizonte, Minas Gerais, Brazil.
 Drawn to Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu in his teenage years, Cassio found inspiration in the early UFCs, watching Royce Gracie overcome much larger opponents through technique and leverage. As a skinny kid, he saw himself in the Gracies, proof that skill could triumph over size. At 19, he finally stepped onto the mats under the legendary Vinícius “Draculino” Magalhães, whose mentorship would shape his journey for decades to come.
 Cassio earned every belt under Draculino’s guidance, culminating in his black belt on December 21, 2010: a testament to their enduring student-mentor bond and Cassio’s dedication to the art.
 A promising competitive career was nearly cut short in 2011 when doctors diagnosed him with three herniated discs in his lower back (L3–L4, L4–L5, and L5–S1), the result of years of intense weightlifting. Two physicians told him he’d never compete again.
 Cassio refused to accept that fate. He took two years off from competition, focusing entirely on healing, rebuilding his body, rethinking his training, and returning with purpose. By late 2013, he was back on the international circuit, quickly reestablishing himself as a dominant force in the heavyweight division and earning major podium finishes.
 Today, Cassio Francis is not only a formidable black belt and competitor, but a revered professor at Trein Club Fort Bend, located in Sugar Land, Houston TX. With a presence that commands both respect and warmth, he guides the next generation of athletes with the same resilience and technical excellence that brought him back from the brink.
 Cassio’s story is more than one of success, it’s one of perseverance, evolution, and belief. At Trein Club Fort Bend, Professor Cassão represents the soul of jiu-jitsu: that even the greatest setbacks can become the foundation of your greatest comeback.

 ### Samuel Garcia
 #### Brown Belt
 Brown Belt (3 stripes) under Professor Pedro Araujo and Cassio Francis.
 Sam started training Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu in January 2018 and has been dedicated to the art ever since. As a blue belt, he began helping teach classes in 2019 and 2020, which sparked his commitment to coaching and mentoring students. In 2020, Sam made the jump to go full time with Jiu-Jitsu and hasn’t looked back since.
 In 2022, he underwent a total ACL reconstruction and focused on recovery with the goal of returning to competition. He made his comeback in 2023, winning gold at the Houston Open and earning third place at the Masters Worlds at purple belt.
 Today, Sam holds a brown belt with three stripes under the guidance of Professor Pedro Araujo and Cassio Francis. He believes you never truly know if you understand a technique or concept until you can teach it to someone else, and he carries that philosophy into every class. Beyond competition, he’s stayed involved in the administrative side of the academy while always keeping a foot on the mats. He’s passionate about pushing toward his black belt and helping others progress on their own journeys in Jiu-Jitsu every day.

 ### Pedro Araújo
 #### 4x BJJ World Champion
 Pedro Araújo was born on October 15, 1989, in Belo Horizonte, Minas Gerais, Brazil. His passion for martial arts began in childhood with Judo, where he developed a strong foundation of discipline and technique in his hometown. In 2009, Pedro transitioned from MMA to Jiu-Jitsu, discovering in the gentle art the path to his true calling. In 2015, he earned his black belt under the guidance of the renowned professor Vinicius "Draculino" Magalhães, one of the most respected figures in Jiu-Jitsu.
 Throughout his competitive journey, Pedro Araújo participated in numerous championships, building a legacy of excellence. He became an eight-time world champion, earned over 40 IBJJF medals, and secured titles in various regional, national, and international competitions. Pedro is widely recognized for his consistency and determination in high-level tournaments, where he faced some of the best athletes in the world, becoming a symbol of resilience and success in the sport.
 As a black belt professor and owner of the Trein academy, Pedro dedicates his life to training athletes and passionate practitioners, promoting physical and mental well-being while spreading Jiu-Jitsu values such as respect, discipline, and humility. With his unique teaching methodology: "ORIENTED, RESPECTFUL, DISCIPLINED, EDUCATED, POWERFUL", he combines advanced techniques, competitive strategies, and a constant focus on the individual development of each student. His approach welcomes everyone, from beginners seeking an introduction to the martial art to advanced athletes aspiring to shine in international competitions.
 Beyond his role as an instructor, Pedro Araújo mentors other professors and practitioners, playing a vital role in the growth of Jiu-Jitsu in his region.
 Recognized not only for his skills on the mat but also for his ability to inspire and motivate, Pedro Araújo is a true example of dedication and passion for the gentle art. He believes that Jiu-Jitsu is not just a fight but a powerful tool for transforming lives, and he is committed to sharing this message with everyone he meets.
 One phrase that has always driven him forward is: "Don’t settle for average."
