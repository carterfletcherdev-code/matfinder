 #### Frequently Asked Questions: ## Do I need experience to start Jiu-Jitsu?
 No experience is required to start Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. Most people who begin training at Renzo Gracie The Woodlands are complete beginners with no martial arts background. Classes are structured to teach fundamentals step by step, and coaches guide you throughout the entire class.
 You don’t need to be in shape or have prior training—just show up ready to learn at your own pace.
 ## What should I wear to my first Jiu-Jitsu class? For your first Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu class, wear comfortable workout clothes such as:
 - A t-shirt or athletic top - Shorts or leggings with no zippers or pockets
 Bring water and a towel. If you don’t have a gi yet, that’s completely fine—you can start without one. Our team will help you choose the right gear if you decide to continue training.
 ## Is Jiu-Jitsu safe for beginners? Yes. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is safe for beginners when taught in a structured, controlled environment. At Renzo Gracie The Woodlands , safety is a top priority—especially for new students.
 Techniques are taught progressively, training is closely supervised, and beginners learn how to tap early, protect themselves, and train responsibly from day one. Jiu-Jitsu emphasizes technique and leverage rather than strength, making it a safe and accessible martial art for beginners of all ages and body types.
 ## What age can kids start Jiu-Jitsu? Children can typically start Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu between ages 4 and 5 , depending on focus and maturity. Our kids Jiu-Jitsu program includes age-specific classes for children ages 4–7 , as well as older age groups.
 Classes are designed to be fun, structured, and safe while helping kids build confidence, discipline, coordination, and respect in a positive environment.
 ## How often should beginners train Jiu-Jitsu? For beginners, training 2–3 times per week is ideal. This allows enough time for recovery while building skills consistently. As students become more comfortable, many choose to train more often, but consistency matters more than intensity.
 Regular training over time leads to the best progress.
 ### Do I need to be in shape to start Jiu-Jitsu? No. Many beginners start Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu with little or no fitness experience. Conditioning improves naturally as you train, and classes are designed to help students progress at a comfortable pace.
 ### Is Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu good for self-defense? Yes. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is one of the most effective martial arts for real-world self-defense, teaching students how to control situations using technique, leverage, and positional awareness rather than size or strength.
