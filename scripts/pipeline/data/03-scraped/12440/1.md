 ### Do I need any experience to start? None at all. Community Week is designed for people on day zero — that's the whole point. You won't be the only beginner in the room.

 ### What do I wear or bring? Just yourself. We'll walk you through everything on your first day. Comfortable athletic clothes are fine to start — shorts and a t-shirt or rash guard.

 ### Is it safe? I'm worried about getting hurt. Safety is the first rule at Identity. Instructors set the pace and no one is pushed beyond their level. Beginner classes are supervised so you're always working with people at the same stage as you.

 ### What happens after Community Week? If you love it, you can continue training for the rest of the month for $97. After that, we'll talk about full membership — no pressure, no hard sell, just a conversation about what works for you.

 ### I'm not athletic. Can I still do this? Yes. Jiu-jitsu is a technique game, not a fitness game — and Community Week is explicitly designed for people starting from zero. If you can get yourself to the gym, you're qualified.

 ### Do you allow drop-ins? Absolutely. Whether you're visiting Seattle for work or just want to check us out, DM us on Instagram to arrange a drop-in. One class or a few — we'll make it work.
