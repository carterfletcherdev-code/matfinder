 ## What to wear
 You can train in a gi if you have one. If not, just come in fitted athletic clothes for your first class and we will supply you with a trial gi. We will help you get everything you need once you are ready to continue.
 If you decide to sign up and keep training, we require all students to wear our team gi. It helps keep the space clean, consistent, and connected. It also reflects the standard we train with together.

 ## No-Gi Jiu-Jitsu Train without the Gi. Focus on control, positioning, and precision.

 In No-Gi, the grips change. But the fundamentals matter just as much as they do in the gi. Without collars or sleeves to hold onto, you rely more on timing, positioning, and body control. The pace is faster, and every adjustment becomes more important.
 No-Gi classes are taught with the same structure and attention to detail as our gi training. You will start with movement drills, work through one or two techniques, and finish with rolling. Each part of class builds on the last, helping you develop real skill through consistent training and calculated adjustments under pressure.
 Training in No-Gi sharpens your timing, improves your sensitivity to movement, and helps you develop cleaner technique. These skills carry over into your gi training and give you a better understanding of what works in both settings.
 It also teaches you how to stay composed when the pace picks up, which is valuable not just in competition, but in real-world situations where control matters.
 Whether you are here to compete, cross-train, or explore another side of Jiu Jitsu, No-Gi will challenge you in ways that help you grow.

 ## Frequently Asked Questions Whether you’re brand new to Jiu-Jitsu or just exploring your options, it’s normal to have questions before stepping on the mat.

 ### Am I too old or out of shape to start?
 No, we have adults of all ages and fitness levels. You’ll start at your pace and build skill and confidence over time.

 ### Will I get hurt doing this?
 There’s always a risk, but safety is built into how we train. You’ll learn with partners who respect control, and we’ll teach you how to train safely and effectively.

 ### What should I expect in my first class?
 You’ll learn basic movements, practice technique, and get comfortable with the pace. We’ll walk you through it so you’ll learn something new and practical from day one.
