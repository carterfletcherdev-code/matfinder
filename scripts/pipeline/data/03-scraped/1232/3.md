 ## Common Questions Do you have Gi and No Gi Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Classes? Yes, we offer both Gi and No-Gi Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu classes. We provide an equal balance of training in both styles, ensuring our students develop a well-rounded skill set. At Creighton MMA, we believe that earning a black belt means being able to perform at a high level in both Gi and No-Gi. Mastery in each form of Jiu-Jitsu is a core part of our philosophy and progression system.

 What makes Creighton MMA different? What makes Creighton MMA different is our family-like atmosphere, world-class instruction, and a genuine passion for helping every student grow—on and off the mat. We combine decades of experience with a supportive, ego-free environment where beginners and seasoned athletes alike feel welcome. Whether you're here to learn self-defense, compete, or just get in shape, you'll be surrounded by coaches and teammates who truly care about your progress.

 How much do classes cost? We have many different packages depending on how often you would like to train and what classes you would like to begin. When you come into your free intro class we will give you a price list and breakdown of all the packages that we offer at Creighton MMA Academy.

 Do I have to compete? No, you are not required to compete. Most of our students sign up at Creighton MMA Academy to get in shape and learn a realistic self defense system at the same time with no intentions to ever compete. With this being said, there are also students that do want to compete. There are many local, state, national and international competitions to compete in each year. We have students that compete in both BJJ and MMA competitions throughout the year.

 Do I have to be in shape to start classes? Absolutely not. The number one reason why students begin their training with us is to get in shape and feel better. Come in and begin training no matter what your fitness level is. We are here to help you reach your fitness goals.

 Is Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu training safe? At Creighton Mixed Martial Arts Academy safety is paramount. Accidents can happen, as in any other sport, but we pay close attention to safety and take extra precautions so that training is not only realistic but safe and enjoyable.
