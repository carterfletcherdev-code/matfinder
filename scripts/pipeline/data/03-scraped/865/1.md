# HOW WE USE “GAMES” TO TEACH JIU JITSU AND WHY IT WORKS!
 Nov 1 Written By Jordan Peitzman

 At Precision Submission Grappling, we take pride in creating an environment where kids not only learn Jiu Jitsu, but also have fun while doing it. One of the core methods we use in our kids’ programs is incorporating “games” into our teaching.
 These aren’t just random activities — they are carefully designed learning experiences based on a constraints-led approach . By shaping the way students move, interact, and solve problems during training, we help them build essential grappling skills naturally and effectively.
 Let’s take a closer look at how this method works and why it’s such a powerful tool for developing confident, capable young grapplers.

 ### THE SCIENCE BEHIND THE FUN At Precision Submission Grappling, our kids’ training methods are based on well-researched approaches that build real skills in a fun and engaging way.
 We use a constraints-led approach , where we adjust the task, environment, or movement options during practice. This encourages students to explore, adapt, and problem-solve — key abilities they’ll need during live grappling situations. Instead of simply memorizing techniques, kids learn how to move, react, and make decisions based on what is happening in real time.
 Our games also help students learn to recognize opportunities for action, build spatial awareness, and develop better timing and control. This creates more natural, instinctive grapplers who can perform under pressure and adapt to any situation.
 By combining fun, dynamic training with proven skill-building strategies, we help students develop both technical ability and strong problem-solving instincts that will serve them in Jiu Jitsu and beyond.

 ### WHAT DO THESE GAMES LOOK LIKE? Games are designed to simulate real grappling scenarios in an engaging and age-appropriate way. They’re performed in a “live” environment, meaning they are done against real resistance and are structured to naturally highlight the key principles of Jiu Jitsu, such as leverage, timing, and control, without overwhelming kids with technical jargon or rigid drills.
 Students are placed in semi-connected positions and given clear and easy-to-understand tasks that guide them towards opportunities for action. Tasks are designed to inform the students about what we want them to do rather than how to do them. This gives students the freedom to explore, experience the effect of their actions, and express their creativity in solving complex grappling problems.

 ### WHY IT WORKS - Engagement: Kids love to play, and games make learning Jiu Jitsu exciting and enjoyable. When kids are having fun, they’re more likely to stay focused and put in the effort to improve. - Retention: Lessons learned through play are more likely to stick. By experiencing Jiu Jitsu concepts firsthand in a game, kids build a deeper understanding of techniques and strategies. - Adaptability: Games expose kids to a wide variety of scenarios, teaching them to adapt and think critically on the fly. This prepares them for live grappling situations where no two situations are ever the same. - Confidence Building: Success in these games boosts self-esteem and helps kids build the resilience they need to tackle challenges on and off the mat.

 ### A BETTER WAY TO LEARN By using games as a teaching tool, we’re not just teaching kids how to grapple, we’re encouraging problem-solving skills, creativity, and a love for learning. This approach not only helps them excel in Jiu Jitsu but also equips them with life skills that will serve them well beyond our academy. You might also be surprised to learn that we use the same approach to learning in our Adult No-Gi Jiu Jitsu classes with tremendous results.
