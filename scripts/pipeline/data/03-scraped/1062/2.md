 ## Techniques You’ll Learn With MMA Training Our MMA classes in Queens, NYC, give students hands-on experience with a variety of martial arts techniques, helping you develop a well-rounded skill set. Each discipline contributes specific tools that make your training practical, dynamic, and effective in real-world scenarios.
 ### Judo Judo emphasizes throws, sweeps, and clinch control. In MMA, it helps students learn how to off-balance opponents, execute takedowns, and control close-range situations. Practicing judo also strengthens timing, coordination, and leverage, all essential for both self-defense and competition.
 ### Wrestling Wrestling teaches takedowns, pins, and positional control. It’s a cornerstone of MMA because it allows fighters to dictate where the fight takes place — standing or on the ground. Students gain strength, balance, and explosive movement skills that translate directly to MMA performance.
 ### Submission Wrestling Submission wrestling focuses on controlling opponents on the ground and applying submission techniques. In our MMA classes, we introduce these concepts as part of grappling strategies, but for a deeper dive into submissions and ground fighting, students can explore our dedicated Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) program . Submission wrestling in MMA serves as a bridge between standing control and advanced BJJ techniques, giving students practical skills they can apply safely during training.
