 FAQ ## Questions from Azusa Residents
 ### How far is 10th Planet Covina from Azusa? About 5 miles and roughly 10 minutes by car. The most direct route is west on Foothill Blvd (Route 66) from downtown Azusa, then south on Citrus Ave — the gym is at 1050 N Citrus Ave. From Azusa Pacific University, you can take Alosta Ave west, which feeds into Foothill Blvd, and you'll be there in about 10–12 minutes. From the I-210, exit at Azusa Ave, head south to Arrow Hwy, and take Citrus Ave north.

 ### Do I need martial arts experience to start? Not at all. The majority of our members walked in as complete beginners. Beginner-friendly classes run in both the morning and evening, and you'll be paired with experienced training partners who meet you at your level. The 10th Planet system is taught progressively, so there's no expectation that you know anything on day one.

 ### Are there programs for kids from Azusa schools? Yes. Our kids classes run Monday through Friday starting at 4 PM — well-timed for students getting out of Azusa High, Gladstone High, Center Middle, Slauson Middle, Valleydale Elementary, and Mountain View Elementary. We run three age groups (3–5, 6–9, and 10–15) so your child trains with kids the same size. Saturday mornings include wrestling for the older kids groups.

 ### Is 10th Planet Jiu Jitsu a good option for APU students? It's one of the best training options near APU for students who want a serious physical activity that's also a genuine martial art. We offer flexible membership options, early morning and late evening classes that work around academic schedules, and a welcoming environment for people who have never trained before. A number of current members are APU students or alumni who started training while in school and stayed with it.

 ### What's the difference between 10th Planet Jiu Jitsu and traditional BJJ? 10th Planet is 100% no-gi — there's no kimono (gi) involved. The system was developed by Eddie Bravo and built specifically for no-gi grappling, with a distinct set of positions and submissions you won't find in traditional BJJ. Because there's no gi to buy or maintain, it's easier and cheaper to get started. Just show up in shorts and a t-shirt or rash guard and you're ready to train.
