 ## Jiu Jitsu
 Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is known as the gentle art. Jiu Jitsu is a martial arts, self defense system, and combat sport. Today it is widely practiced by our military and law enforcement due to its superior grappling application, making it easy for anyone to use. Jiu Jitsu is meant to be efficient for anyone to use. It allows smaller people to be able to beat larger, stronger, and faster opponents by utilizing proper leverage, body mechanics, and technique.

 Gi (Uniform) - Gi Jiu Jitsu is where you wear a Japanese-style Kimono. The Gi allows you and your opponent to make grips on their clothing that can be used as a weapon. Collar chokes and sleeve grips can give you the edge when grappling with a uniform.
 ​
 NoGi - It is exactly how it sounds, No (without the) Gi. In this class practitioners wear a rashguard and shorts. In this class you learn how to grapple without the use of the uniform.

 ## Muay Thai
 Muay Thai, also referred to as Thai Boxing, is the art of eight limbs. Muay Thai is a striking martial art and combat sport that incorporates punches, kicks, knees, elbows, and clinching.
 ​
 Whether you're interested in learning the basics and getting in shape or eventually fighting at competitive level, we have the experience, connections, and curriculum to get you there.

 ## Do I need to get in shape to start?
 No, nothing will get you ready for Jiu Jitsu or Muay Thai except Jiu Jitsu or Muay Thai itself. It is a misconception to want to "get in shape" before beginning martial arts training, but there isn't an exercise that can replicate actual martial arts training.
