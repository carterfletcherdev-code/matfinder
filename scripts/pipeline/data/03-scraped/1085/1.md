 Brazilian jiu-jitsu; noun, (Portuguese: jiu-jitsu brasileiro)
 A self-defense martial art and combat sport based on grappling, ground fighting, and submission holds. It focuses on taking one's opponent down to the ground, using several techniques to force them into submission.

 Frequently Asked Questions

 Do I need to be in shape to start Jiu Jitsu?

 No, not at all! We understand that everyone is at different starting points. Our instructors and students will match your pace, and you can take a break whenever you need.
 Is Jiu-Jitsu safe to train as a beginner?
 It is safe to start training, even as a complete beginner. Everyone at G-Town knows to train safely and is taught how to do so. We openly invite every student to stop if they are stuck or feel unsafe.
 How long does it take to progress in Jiu Jitsu?
 That depends on how much you consistently show up. If you want to progress faster all it takes is putting in the effort and being here. Some gyms progress you based on an unpersonalized attendance rule. At G-Town we watch your individual progress closely to determine when you’ve reached the next step.
 Do I need to have my own gear?
 We provide all the equipment you need including gis, belts, gloves, pads, and water. Just bring some clothes to work out in!
