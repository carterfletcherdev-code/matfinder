## DT Jiu Jitsu ## Frequently Asked Questions

 ####
 #### 1️⃣ Do I need experience to start? No. Zero experience? Perfect. You’ll fit right in.
 - We’ve designed our classes to meet you where you’re at.
 - Your first day? We guide you, pair you with the right partners, and keep it beginner-friendly.
 - No one expects you to be a pro—just show up, learn, and improve.
 👉 First class is free. No excuses. Book it now .

 #### 2️⃣ What do I wear to class? Depends on the class:
 - Gi Classes: Bring a clean BJJ Gi & belt (or rent one here).
 - No-Gi & Wrestling: Rashguard, no-pocket shorts, compression pants (optional).
 - For every class: Flip-flops (mats stay clean), deodorant (respect your training partners).
 👉 Not sure? Just wear athletic gear and we’ll help you out.

 #### 3️⃣ Do I need to be in shape to start Jiu Jitsu? Nope. Jiu-Jitsu gets you in shape—being in shape is not a requirement.
 - Every class is designed to push you at your own pace.
 - You’ll get stronger, move better, and build cardio naturally.
 - Most people start thinking they need to be fit—they get fit by training.
 👉 Best time to start? Now. Book your first class .

 #### 4️⃣ Will I get hurt? BJJ is a controlled combat sport. If you train smart, injuries are rare.
 - We teach safety-first training.
 - You’ll roll with partners who match your level.
 - Tapping (submitting) = you control when to stop. No ego, no pressure.
 👉 We train hard, but we train safe. Come try it yourself .

 #### 5️⃣ How long does a class last? Most classes run about 60 minutes —short, sharp, and structured:
 - Warm-up (movement & mobility)
 - Technique (drill it till it sticks)
 - Live Training (rolling & application)
 👉 Two hours of free parking. You’ll be in and out with time to spare.

 #### 6️⃣ What if I feel awkward my first day? You won’t. Here’s why:
 - Everyone starts as a beginner. No one expects you to know anything.
 - Our team makes sure you feel welcome. No cliques, no egos—just training.
 - You’ll leave better than you walked in. Even on day one.
 👉 Forget the nerves. The hardest part is showing up.

 #### 7️⃣ What’s the best way to start? Simple: Just show up.
 - Step 1: Book your first class.
 - Step 2: Walk through the door.
 - Step 3: Train, learn, improve.
 No long-term contracts. No hidden fees. Just real training in a serious but welcoming environment.
 👉 Your first class is free . No excuses. Lock it in.
