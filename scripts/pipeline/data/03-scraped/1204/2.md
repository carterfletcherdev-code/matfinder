 # "Why Should I Learn Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, And What Can BJJ Training Do For Me?"
 Dear Reader,

 Here’s a proven FACT about fighting:

 Most—almost ALL—fights end up on the ground (or in grappling range) at some point during the fight.

 So that means...

 If you can’t defend yourself on the ground, YOU CAN’T FIGHT!

 Bottom line is, if you don’t know what to do in a fight on the ground, you’re putting yourself at risk, and even your loved ones! That sounds unpleasant, but it’s the truth.

 The fact you’re on this page right now means you’re at least somewhat serious about learning more about how to defend yourself, and learning the tried-and-tested, proven successful techniques of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu.

 Maybe you want to compete in the sport, or maybe you just want to get the benefits of training and learning this powerful martial art… either way, we’ve got you covered!

 Let’s face it. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a “must-know” style for any professional mixed martial artists like the ones fighting in the UFC (Ultimate Fighting Championship).

 And these days, BJJ should most definitely be a “must-know” for pretty much ANYONE.

 Here’s why:

 The fight-controlling positions of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, along with the fight-ending submissions, have been battle-tested in combat, in sport, in the cage (MMA), and on the street.

 And probably the main reason that Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is something EVERYONE should learn, is because it can be effective regardless of a person’s size or strength!

 BJJ was developed so that a smaller, weaker person can successfully defend himself or herself against a larger, stronger, heavier opponent.

 That’s why ANYONE can do Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, and that’s why EVERYONE should learn this incredible martial art!

 With Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, the smaller person really CAN beat the bigger person!

 Here’s how a person who knows Brazilian Jiu Jitsu can defeat a bigger, stronger attacker:

 It’s done by taking the fight to the ground and using proper technique and leverage, and then applying joint locks or chokeholds to defeat the resisting opponent, making them quit, and getting yourself out of harm’s way.

 What Is Brazilian Jiu Jitsu?

 “Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is a sport or activity where you learn to use your body as a tool to control and ultimately submit your opponent using techniques and leverage on the ground and sometimes standing as well.” — Brazilian Jiu Jitsu Black Belt Matt Leighton, Citadel BJJ Head Coach

 Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, or BJJ for short, is a grappling-based martial art and complete self defense system.

 This system has been PROVEN time and time again, in REAL FIGHTS, for years now.

 Although Brazilian Jiu Jitsu was designed for fighting and self defense, it has since evolved into a sport, with specific rules. Now, athletes of all types and all skill levels have the opportunity to compete against each other in structured events like local and national tournaments, and even the world championships. There are competitions with and without the uniform, which is called a gi, or kimono. Almost all BJJ tournaments do not allow striking, so the matches are often a combination of wrestling, Judo, and of course, Jiu Jitsu.

 Here at Citadel BJJ in Iowa City, we have a team with a lot of competitors. However, lots of our students don’t compete—they are training “for life”!

 So, if you don’t want to compete, we won’t force you to compete!

 BJJ also includes striking techniques, takedowns, throws, and stand-up techniques and strategies that enable people to handle themselves in a real fight.

 That being said, the daily classes at Citadel BJJ do not feature kicking or punching. The focus is on safe grappling technique that can be done on a daily basis with no more fear of injury than any other contact sport.

 Why Brazilian Jiu Jitsu Was Created

 Brazilian Jiu Jitsu was created to allow a smaller, weaker person to defend himself or herself against a bigger, stronger, more athletic opponent by using proper technique and leverage. This is achieved primarily by taking the fight to the ground.

 A small person who knows the proper techniques and positioning from BJJ can beat a bigger person who doesn’t know technique. Jiu Jitsu is one of the only martial arts where that’s REAL!

 Here’s what makes Brazilian Jiu Jitsu such an effective martial art:

 By closing the distance and taking the fight to the ground, your opponent’s abilities to strike and inflict damage are greatly reduced.

 The strategy is primarily to take away your opponents’ strengths and ability to win, while applying your own.

 Here’s why it’s usually better to take the fight to the ground (especially if you know BJJ):

 The longer you stand and trade punches with someone who’s bigger and stronger, the higher their chances are of hitting you with a damaging strike. The stronger your opponent, the harder they will hit. The bigger the opponent, the more damage they can take, and the longer their reach is, so they can hit you without getting hit. So bigger reach and powerful strikes are taken away when grappling on the ground.

 Jiu Jitsu is a methodical self defense and fighting system that consists of these four key strategies:

 - 1. Closing the distance - 2. Taking the fight to the ground - 3. Achieving a dominant position - 4. Finishing the fight

 At Citadel Brazilian Jiu Jitsu, you’ll become proficient in all four of these crucial stages of a fight.
