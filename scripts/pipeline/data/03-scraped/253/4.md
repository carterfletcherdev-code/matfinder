 The Art
 ## Train With Purpose Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu was created by the Gracie family, who modified judo and traditional Japanese jiu-jitsu into a discipline built on leverage over strength. At Espiritu, we carry that lineage forward with focused instruction, a welcoming environment, and programs designed for every stage of life.

 Heritage
 ## The Lineage Every instructor at Espiritu Jiu-Jitsu carries a direct lineage to the pioneers who brought this art from Japan to Brazil and to the world.

 c. 1878 – 1941
 #### Mitsuyo Maeda A master of Kodokan Judo who traveled to Brazil in 1914, where he taught the art that would become Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu.

 1902 – 1994
 #### Carlos Gracie Sr. Student of Maeda. Founded the first Gracie Jiu-Jitsu academy in Rio de Janeiro in 1925.

 1913 – 2009
 #### Hélio Gracie Adapted techniques for a smaller frame, emphasizing leverage over strength — the foundation of modern BJJ.

 Born 1956
 #### Carlos Gracie Jr. Founded the International Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Federation. Expanded BJJ across the world.

 Born 1967
 #### Renzo Gracie Legendary competitor and coach. Pioneer of BJJ in the United States and mixed martial arts.

 5th Degree Black Belt
 #### Shawn Williams Black Belt under Renzo Gracie. Creator of the Williams Guard. Decades of competition and instruction.

 Black Belt · Founder
 #### Jeremy Espiritu Black Belt under Shawn Williams. Founded Espiritu Jiu-Jitsu in Oxnard, California. Competitor at IBJJF Worlds and Pans.
