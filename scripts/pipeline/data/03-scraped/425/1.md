 ## Who make it happen

 ## Professor Mineiro

 Head Coach

 Marcelo began training in 1999 in Minas Gerais state of Brazil. When he moved to Sao Paulo in 2003, he joined TT (the Terere/Telles Team) where he shared the mats with world champions Rubens Cobrinha and Andre Galvao. In 2006, Marcelo joined the Checkmat team with Leo Vieira and later received his black belt in 2008.
 Since then, his coaching led him to train teammates in Ireland, Finland, Poland, and in the UK. In 2014, he joined Professor Leandro Vieira and provided training for the UFC fighters at AKA (American Kickboxing Academy) in San Jose, CA. Today he has dedicated himself to his two academies in the Bay Area cities of Emeryville and Alamo, California.

 ## Ray Juachon

 Coach

 Ray began his Jiu Jitsu journey in 2018 under Professor Mineiro and Professor Liz, and is currently a brown belt teaching both adults and kids classes at the Alamo gym. Throughout his time training under both professors, Ray has been an active competitor on the local and international scene, winning world titles such as:
 - 2025 IBJJF NoGi Worlds, Brown 1st place - 2025 IBJJF World Master, Brown belt 2nd place - 2024 IBJJF NoGi Worlds, Purple belt 1st place - 2023 IBJJF NoGi Worlds, Purple belt 2nd place - 2022 IBJJF World Master, Blue belt 1st place
 Ray is a life-long martial artist with experience in Muay Thai, kickboxing, and Filipino Martial Arts.

 ## Professor Liz

 Head Coach

 Professor Liz is a 1st degree Brazilian Jiu Jitsu Black Belt with nearly two decades of experience on the mat. She earned her Brown Belt in 2014 from World Champion Lucas Leite and was promoted to Black Belt in 2019 by Master Leandro Vieira.
 She leads as Head Coach at Mineiro BJJ Emeryville since its opening in 2021. As a dedicated competitor, Liz earned 2nd place at the 2024 IBJJF No-Gi World Championships and 2nd place at the 2023 IBJJF Master International North America.
 Her classes are designed to provide complete experience for each student, offering both beginner and advanced modifications to support all levels of practice.
 Mineiro BJJ proudly follows official IBJJF rules and guidelines, including belt progression and the scoring system. Safety and structure are always emphasized in every class.

 ## Frequently asked Questions

 What Should I Bring to My First Jiu Jitsu Class?
 Wear comfortable athletic clothing and bring a water bottle to stay hydrated.

 Is Brazilian Jiu Jitsu suitable for beginners?
 Yes, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is suitable for beginners of all ages and fitness levels.

 Do I need to be in shape to start training Brazilian Jiu Jitsu?
 No, Brazilian Jiu Jitsu is for everyone, regardless of current fitness level.

 I'm worried about getting injured. Is Brazilian Jiu Jitsu safe?
 Brazilian Jiu Jitsu emphasizes technique and control, reducing the risk of injury.

 Can Brazilian Jiu Jitsu help with self-defense?
 Absolutely. Brazilian Jiu Jitsu teaches effective self-defense techniques for individuals of all sizes and strengths.

 What can I expect in my first Brazilian Jiu Jitsu class?
 In your first class, you’ll start with a warm-up followed by instruction on basic techniques and positions.

 I'm not interested in competing. Can I still train Brazilian Jiu Jitsu?
 Yes, many practitioners train for self-improvement, fitness, and camaraderie without competing.

 What if I'm a parent considering enrolling my child in Brazilian Jiu Jitsu?
 Brazilian Jiu Jitsu can benefit children by promoting discipline, confidence, and physical fitness. Our academy offers a safe environment with experienced instructors adept at engaging children effectively.
