About this gym

10th Planet Austin is the city's longest-running No-Gi-only academy. Founded in 2014, the gym focuses on the 10th Planet system pioneered by Eddie Bravo, with a strong competitor pipeline and a beginner-friendly fundamentals track. Saturday open mats are free for visiting practitioners with a rashguard.

Photo strategy — the key insight

You're right that bad photos drag the site down. Here's the rule I'd code into the page:

Photo sourceTreatmentRationale
Gym uploaded 1+ photos (claimed) Photo hero (Variant 1) The gym chose this photo. They own the brand impression.
Gym uploaded 4+ photos (claimed, active) Mosaic grid (Variant 3) Multiple photos average out, more authentic, browseable gallery.
No gym-uploaded photos (default / unclaimed) Monogram fallback (Variant 2) We don't show the Google photo unless it's high quality. The branded fallback looks intentional, not lazy. Adds an "Add photos" CTA that converts gym owners.
Google photo only, exists, low confidence Monogram fallback (Variant 2) Same as no-photo — we don't trust Google photos by default.
Google photo only, exists, high confidence Photo hero with subtle "Photo from Google · click to update" tag Edge case. Use only when we can verify the photo is gym interior + decent resolution.

The cheap default for now: show the monogram fallback (V2) by default. Show photos only when the gym is claimed AND has uploaded their own. This protects the site's look 100% and gives gyms a reason to claim ($30/mo Featured). Photos become a paid-tier signal, not a default.

One more piece I'd add: "Quick verify" for travelers

Travelers most often want to know: can I just show up tomorrow? Right now they have to read several sections to figure that out. I'd add a small green callout below the openmat-hero panel ONLY for travelers (when distance > 50 mi):

Visiting Austin?
No reservation needed. Free for drop-ins with rashguard. Saturday is the easiest day.

Easy to wire when the user's location and the gym's drop-in policy are known. Skippable for v1 if it complicates things.
